Exploring how Canadian energy, LNG, and artificial intelligence can expand access to reliable power worldwide.

What is Energy Poverty

Energy poverty refers to the lack of reliable, affordable, and safe access to energy services such as electricity, heating, and clean cooking fuels. According to global development organizations like Habitat for Humanity, energy poverty affects millions of families who must choose between paying energy bills and meeting other basic needs.

The International energy community, including the International Energy Agency, reports that hundreds of millions of people worldwide still lack access to electricity. Without reliable energy, communities face limited healthcare access, reduced educational opportunities, and slower economic growth.


Energy access is more than convenience, it is directly connected to opportunity, stability, and human development.
