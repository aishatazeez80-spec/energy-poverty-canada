LNG Explained

What is LNG

Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) is natural gas that has been cooled to approximately -162°C, turning it into liquid form. This process reduces its volume significantly, allowing it to be transported by ship to countries without pipeline connections.

Liquefying LNG

The liquefaction process involves removing impurities such as water and carbon dioxide before gradually cooling the gas to extremely low temperatures. Once condensed into liquid form, LNG is stored in insulated tanks and loaded onto specialized carriers. Facilities such as LNG Canada process Canadian natural gas for export, connecting domestic production to international markets.

Global Distribution

Globally, LNG plays an important role in energy distribution and energy security. It allows countries without significant natural gas reserves to access reliable fuel supplies and diversify their energy mix. In some cases, natural gas is used to replace coal in electricity generation, which can reduce certain greenhouse gas emissions.
